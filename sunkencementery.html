About Sunken Cemetery

The Sunken Cemetery is one of Camiguin’s most famous historical attractions. It was formed after the eruption of Mount Vulcan in the 1870s, which caused the old cemetery of Bonbon to sink beneath the sea.

Today, a large cross stands above the water to mark the site, creating a peaceful and hauntingly beautiful landmark. Visitors can take a boat to the cross, snorkel around the area, or enjoy the sunset view from the shore.
